Satellite crop segmentation

The challenge

Before any model could learn, the ground truth had to exist. Twenty-one sites, different crops and seasons, and satellite scenes where clouds and shadows quietly corrupt every mask they touch.

No dataset existed

Labels and masks had to be constructed from raw satellite imagery before segmentation work could even begin.

21 sites, one system

Soils, crops, and seasons vary site to site — a single pipeline had to hold up across all of them.

Clouds and shadows lie

Occlusions corrupt training labels silently. They had to be detected and masked with confidence, not ignored.

The approach

Build the dataset, clean the sky out of it, train the segmentation models — and package everything so the client's team could run with it.

01 — DATASET

Dataset construction

Built the training corpus from raw satellite imagery across all 21 sites — scene selection, labeling strategy, and quality control.

02 — CLEANING

Cloud & shadow masking

Segmented cloud and shadow with per-region confidence scores, so contaminated pixels are excluded from training instead of silently poisoning it.

03 — MODELS

Field & crop segmentation

Trained the segmentation models for field-boundary extraction and crop classification across the full site diversity.

04 — DELIVERY

Handoff-ready packaging

Delivered the pipeline end-to-end — documented and packaged for the client team to operate and extend without me in the loop.

Outcomes

From zero dataset to an operating segmentation system, handed off clean.
